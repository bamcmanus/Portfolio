About Me

Brent McManus is a California transplant who moved to Oregon in 2002 when he enrolled at the University of Oregon. He graduated with a Bachelor of Science in human physiology in 2006. His passion in the field led him to open a CrossFit gym. Life is crazy and plans change, however. His next adventure took him to Fort Polk Louisiana, where he spent seven years as an infantryman in the United States Army. He completed two deployments to Afghanistan where he learned that all the "stresses of life" truly don't matter. Life is short and you have to enjoy what you do. Because of this, after his time in the military, he decided to pursue his interest in computer programming. His spouse's family brought he and his family back to Oregon, this time Portland. He plans to complete his Masters of Science in computer science in June of 2019. He contributes to "keeping it weird" by shooting his bow, visiting Rose City Comic-Con in cosplay with his family, cheering on the Timebers and Thorns, spending time with his daughters at Rose City Futsal and painfully enduring Bravo TV due to his wife's obsession.
